About Me

Michael Meisel is a technologist, team builder, and artist who is passionate about finding simple solutions to complex problems. He has spent his career helping startups build their core technologies and teams from the ground up. He has built everything from interactive computer network visualizations to zero-maintenance, solar-powered PCs for rural areas in the developing world.

As an artist, Michael is interested in making tangible, technological artifacts that are indistinguishable from magic. This presents opportunities to showcase the power of technology as an instrument of both good and evil. His aesthetic is best described as minimalist.

Michael holds a Ph.D. in Computer Science from UCLA (with a focus in network protocols) and a B.A. in Computer Science from UC Berkeley.

Current Work

I am currently available for advisory positions or consulting, particular for non-profits and social enterprises, but for young startups as well.

Artistically, I am currently working with the Gray Area Foundation for the Arts, both as a course instructor for their educational program and as a member of their artist incubator. I am also interested in artistic collaborations and helping artists to incorporate physical computing into their work.
